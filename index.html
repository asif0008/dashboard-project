How can I increase my return on marketing investment?

Marketing Is Classified Into Two Types. The First Is The Brand, And The Second Is Everything Else. We Say This Because Quantifying A Brand Is Difficult.

We Advocate Allocating A Budget For Brand Marketing With The Expectation That It Will Be Valuable Even If It Cannot Be Measured.

Every Other Marketing Program Should Be Observed And Evaluated For Its Return On Investment. The Most Typical Mistake That Novice Marketers Make Is To Deduct The Expense Of A Program From The Income It Earned.

To Calculate The ROI Correctly, Deduct The Project's Cost From The Gross Profit Margin, Not The Total Revenue.

For $150,000 In Revenue, A 50% Gross Profit Margin, And A $30,000 Marketing Investment, A Highly Simple Formula Would Be As Follows:

(Gross Profit Minus Investment) = ($75,000 - $30,000).
